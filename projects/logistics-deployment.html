02 Project Management · Logistics DX

$6M Logistics
Deployment

A full logistics system implementation — completed one week ahead of a tight three-month deadline.

The What

End-to-end management of a $6M SGD logistics system contract at ST Logistics — from scope alignment through technical coordination, stakeholder sign-offs, and go-live. The engagement spanned a new client account and involved coordinating between internal operations, IT, and an external technology vendor under a three-month delivery window.

The project was delivered on time and within budget, with final handover completed one week ahead of the contracted deadline.

The Why

Logistics technology deployments are notoriously prone to scope creep and integration delays. The technical requirements are real but the harder problem is usually alignment: different teams have different definitions of "done," and a three-month timeline leaves almost no slack for renegotiation.

My focus throughout was on making requirements explicit early, surface ambiguity before it became conflict, and maintain clear ownership of each workstream so nothing drifted between handoffs. The early delivery was a direct result of front-loading the alignment work that typically gets deferred.

What I Learned

  • The "implementation gap" is a people problem, not a technology problem. The system itself was capable. The delays in comparable projects come from technical requirements that weren't translated into operational language — and vice versa. I spent significant time in that translation layer.

  • Compressed timelines reward ruthless prioritisation. Three months is short enough that you cannot recover lost weeks. I learned to distinguish between what had to be true at go-live versus what could be iterated afterward — and to make that distinction explicit with the client.

  • Early delivery is a trust deposit. Coming in a week ahead of schedule on a flagship account shaped every subsequent conversation with that client. Reliability compounds.
