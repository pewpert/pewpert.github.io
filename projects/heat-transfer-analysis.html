Heat Transfer
Analysis

Modelling heat transfer in a rotor-stator spinning disc reactor — research conducted at the Technical University of Munich.

The What

A data analysis project conducted at TU Munich (Dec 2018 – Mar 2019) as part of my Bachelor's in Chemical Engineering. The work involved processing experimental data from heat transfer trials run on a rotor-stator spinning disc reactor — a compact, high-shear device used in chemical processing.

Using MATLAB, I built a processing pipeline to clean the raw sensor data, compute relevant heat transfer coefficients, and fit the results to theoretical models. The goal was to characterise how well the reactor transferred heat across different operating conditions — speed, flow rate, and temperature differential.

The Why

Spinning disc reactors are interesting because their performance is highly dependent on fluid dynamics that are difficult to predict from first principles alone. The experimental characterisation work fills that gap — giving engineers empirical data they can use to design and scale processes with confidence.

For me, this project was an early lesson in the gap between clean theoretical models and messy real-world data. Sensor noise, outliers, and edge-case operating conditions meant the analysis required judgment at every step — not just code.

What I Learned

  • Data cleaning is where the real analysis happens. The modelling step was straightforward once the data was trustworthy. Understanding what to exclude and why — rather than just dropping outliers mechanically — required reading the physics closely.

  • Visualisation drives insight before statistics do. Plotting the raw data before fitting any model revealed patterns — and anomalies — that I would have missed if I had gone straight to regression.

  • Engineering and data science are the same discipline at the boundary. This project set the foundation for how I think about analytical work: start with the physical reality, let the data reflect it, and be sceptical of results that seem too clean.
