Retro
Dashboard

Turning sprint retrospectives into insight that compounds over time.

The What

Retro Dashboard is a lightweight web app for capturing, tagging, and tracking retrospective items across sprints. Each item — whether a "What went well," "What didn't," or "Action" — is tagged by theme (process, communication, tooling, etc.) and persists between sessions.

Over time, a simple chart shows which themes keep resurfacing. If "unclear requirements" appears in five consecutive retros, the dashboard makes that pattern undeniable — which makes the conversation with leadership much shorter.

The Why

Retrospectives felt like the most honest meeting on the calendar, and also the least leveraged. We'd fill a Miro board, agree on three actions, and close the tab. Two weeks later, nobody could remember what we'd said. The actions lived in the meeting notes nobody re-read.

I wanted a tool that treated retro data as a longitudinal dataset, not a one-off output. The insight isn't in any single retro — it's in the pattern across twenty of them.

What I Learned

  • Taxonomy is product design. The tag list I chose shaped what teams noticed. When I added "morale" as a tag, people started tagging things they'd previously left untagged. The categories create the language.

  • Local storage is underrated for low-stakes tools. I spent a week planning a backend before realising the entire audience was one team, in one browser. localStorage solved 90% of the problem with 10% of the effort.

  • Data without a decision point is just trivia. The chart showing recurring themes was interesting. But it only changed behaviour once I added a simple prompt: "This theme has appeared 4 times. What's the root cause?" The question is what makes the data actionable.
